Alistair Sutherland is a baritone living and working in London.

He has completed the MA vocal studies course at the Royal Academy of Music under Alex Ashworth. He is now a pupil of Nicholas Powell.

He has worked extensively with the company OperaUpClose recently performing the title role in The Marriage of Figaro, Schaunard in their award winning La bohème, and Dulcamara in their critically acclaimed L'elisir d'amore.

He began with the Opera Holland Park chorus in 2010 where he was selected as as 2012 Christie Collins Young Artist performing in Gianni Schicchi. He continues to work with the company performing-lgall roles and covers. Recently he has worked with OHP Inspire performing The Pirate King (Pirates of Penzance) as part of their outreach programme.

Alistair works with Freathy Tippet as an associate artist, devising and performing new works. He receives coaching in acting and movement from company director Julie -lgan. As a recitalist Alistair works regularly with Horacio López Redondo performing a variety of repertoire from Tchaikovsky to Peter Dickinson.

Future plans include the reprisal of several roles with OperaUpClose as they tour regional theatres across the UK.
